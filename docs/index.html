About EuroCIM

The general theme of the meeting is “Causal Inference in Health, Economics, and Social Sciences”. Causal inference is broadly defined, and the focus is on statistical methodology and challenging applications that highlight necessary methodological extensions.

  • EuroCIM aims to provide a forum for people interested in causal inference to meet informally, for early career and established researchers to present and discuss the latest developments in the field, and to offer opportunities for networking to develop future research opportunities and collaborations.
  • EuroCIM evolved from what used to be the “UK Causal Inference Meetings” (UK-CIM). The EuroCIM steering committee consists of Alessandra Mattei, Rhian Daniel, Erin Gabriel, Fabrizia Mealli, Finbarr Leacy, Jon Michael Gran, Karla Diaz-Ordaz, Kate Tilling, Kjetil Roysland, Mats Stensrud, Niels Richard Hansen, Noemi Kreif, Oliver Dukes, Paul Clarke, Richard Emsley, Sara Geneletti, Sabine Landau, Michele Santacatterina, Stephen Burgess, and Vanessa Didelez.

Please note that registration for the meeting is not restricted to people from Europe, and we welcome participation from anyone who would like to attend!

Methodological challenges in using electronic health record data to estimate conditional average treatment effects

We will have the following format:

  • Overview of key challenges in estimating conditional average treatment effects (CATE) in EHR data (Elizabeth, ~25 mins)
  • Methodological issues, including recoverability of CATE and assumptions about mechanism shift (Johan, ~40 mins)
  • Application to the estimation of comparative effectiveness of SGLT2i vs DPP4i among patients with diabetes in the Clinical Practice Research Datalink (Elizabeth, ~15 mins)
  • Questions/discussion (Both speakers)

Ideas for defining and implementing interventions in continuous-time event history settings

We will have two talks of equal length, with time for questions after each talk.

  • Pål will start the session by speaking about: “Interventions, Potential Outcomes, and Identification of Causal Effects in Marked Point Processes”.
  • Helene will follow and speak about: “Calibrated stochastic scaling interventions on time-to-event processes”.

Interference and complex treatments

We will have two talks of equal length, with time for questions at the end.

  • Fredrik will start the session by speaking about: “Coupling Designs for Randomized Experiments with Complex Treatments”.
  • Cory will follow and speak about: “Complex Treatments in Environmental Science: Interference and Spatial Confounding”.
